There are some maxims that I try to live by which I often find myself repeating to my friends. They are ingrained in my brain as obvious truths because of the kind of content I swim around in, but not so obvious to others standing at the shores. Me writing them down doesn’t mean that I’m the living embodiment of these, but these are some things I *hope* to live by. So here goes

**Disclaimer**: Most of them come from [Visa](https://visakanv.com/). This man has infused my brain with so many good brain worms and has completely changed the way I live my life. 

## 1. You can iterate towards greatness and it’s worth iterating towards.

At least in my age, I would say it’s fair to be delusional about your prospects of achieving greatness. You can be great at whatever craft you are practicing. But greatness can only be iterated towards. There’s no surefire way to achieve that in one shot as far as I know. The iterations will take years of your life but it’ll be worth it if you love your craft enough to do it even when you suck at it. 

## 2. Put work out into the public often and optimize for being prolific rather than good.

To iterate towards greatness you need to keep doing the work and putting it out. It probably will not be as good as you want it to be. There’ll be skill issues, there’ll be [your work lagging behind your taste](https://www.goodreads.com/author/quotes/113989.Ira_Glass) issues. But there needs to be a body of work you can point to and say “I did that”. You have to own up to how shitty it is while also being proud of yourself for having the determination and courage of doing it and making it public. Each iteration will be better than the previous one. 
I can't quite put it into words, but there's something about putting your bad attempts in public which feels therapeutic. It feels like unclogging a stream. "Shipping something" has a certain ring to it which I like.  
Also, one another reason of why I'm such a big proponent of putting out work in public is that the thing that has inspired me the most in recent times are my friends' unfinished and unpublisehd drafts and drawings. I would have loved if more of it was on the Internet. 
More reading on this - https://www.visakanv.com/blog/do100things/

## 3. Build supportive contexts
*Sometimes people think their bottleneck is time but it’s actually the absence of a supportive context - Visa*

Life is just more fun in multi player mode. I’m not saying you should only surround yourself with people who have exactly the same goals as you (could lead to echo chambers), but having an environment in which people are openly ambitious and are taking steps in service to those ambitions and helping each other out is just incredibly rewarding. Such an environment could be hard to build because it requires solving for these things:

a. Having an ambition which requires some introspection and articulating your vectors

b. Having the type of friends who you feel comfortable around enough to share your ambitions

c. Having friends who are introspective enough to have ambitions

d. Getting rid of the default scarcity mindset which makes you look at life as a zero sum game. It’s hard to get rid of but once you do the beauty is just immeasurable. **It’s just fun to be surrounded but winners**. By winners, I mean people who have goals and are striving to achieve them.

## 4. Learn to give yourself rewards outside of work
The reason we sometimes refrain from working on our personal projects is because our body has learnt that there might not be an appropriate reward at the end of a slog. If the personal project is not gratifying in itself, then this will backfire for sure. The only place where most of us are getting rewarded is at our work where the systems are continuously being optimized to give you the least reward per unit of work you do - nothing wrong in that, this is what an efficient entity should be doing. But this also means that we need to get good setting up our own reward systems. If not, we'll become slaves to the reward systems built by others. 
I think one of the things I like about "putting work out in public" is that sharing your questionable quality work out in public is sometimes rewarding enough in itself. It motivates you to do more of it. It's actually the reason I'm putting out my bad writing here and my [bad photography out there on Instagram.](https://www.instagram.com/a__bhimany_u?igsh=MTg2bDJjMWc4YXRkYw%3D%3D&utm_source=qr)
Other ways of giving yourself rewards can be:
- Celebrating the win with friends by throwing a small party.
- Buying something for yourself.
Open to more suggestions here. These are the ones I could think of from the top of my head.

## 5. Slow is smooth, smooth is fast
I’ve written about this [before](https://gravity-well.netlify.app/posts/speed/) but I’ll reiterate. Every time I’ve tried to exert speed in terms of amount of work I do in a given unit of time or the number of things I do, the output has been really abysmal (funny how LLMs optimize the same thing with similar tradeoffs). It’s only when I have slowed down to really pay attention, to get my nervous system in tune to rhythms of the task I’m doing is when I have produced something I’m proud of. Spending enough time in this state leads to speed eventually - when you are surfing the waves of momentum, the flow state. It's beautiful but unfortunately it can't be forced.

## 6. Collaborate
I have yet to seriously do this with someone other than Judah, but this sounds fun. I think doing projects with friends could be very fun and rewarding. Gives you more of an excuse to spend time together and actually feel the crevices of each other's brain. Will try and do more of it.
